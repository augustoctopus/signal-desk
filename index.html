What it does, concretely

One config file in — a territory definition. Out: every institution that fits, scored for AI-buying readiness against 18 months of public record with a source on every claim, plus buying-committee maps, discovery briefs, and drafted outreach in two test variants. A week of analyst work in about an hour, re-runnable weekly. It stops before sending — that's a human decision.

My read of the role

The JD's core sentence is turning product usage, pilots and inbound demand into qualified, high-conviction opportunities. That's a conversion motion, not cold outbound — so I built a conversion engine. From outside, public signals (hiring velocity, exec intent, LLM footprint, regulatory posture, transformation programs) are proxies for the product signal OpenAI holds internally. Inside the building, the same pipeline runs on the real thing and gets an order of magnitude sharper.

The experiment

Two outreach theories — signal-mirror vs peer-proof — assigned at account level, instrumented end to end. Detecting a 5%→8% reply lift needs ~1,059 accounts per arm, which is why this demo claims no results: the experiment is designed, not performed. Honesty is cheaper than a fake win rate.
